Computer clinic training event

5pm - 7pm Tuesday 3rd May

LF15, Kilburn building

Call for volunteers: help out at a computer clinic for all students at the University of Manchester.

Have you ever looked at a humanities student's laptop? You may thinks it's funny, but these people are suffering and we having the skills to help them. So, à la Médecins Sans Frontières, we will be cross the border of Kilburn into the Student Union to give philanthropic technical aid help those most in need.

Man-UP will be running a free Computer Clinic for all students. It will be our job to help address the many ailments of the laptops that come through the door. The clinic session will be on Wednesday 4th May from 12pm - 3pm. We'll be setting up from 11am.

If you'd like help out, we're running a training session on Tuesday 3rd May from 5pm - 7pm in LF15. We'll be going over the software arsenal and other various bits of equipment we'll be using to battle the forces of computer corruption. Anyone is welcome to volunteer to help, some knowledge of Windows or Macs is preferred, e.g., how to change the power settings/uninstall a programme.
