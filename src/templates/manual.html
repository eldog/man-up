Manual

This manual is designed to contain all the necessary information regarding the structure of Manchester Ultimate Programming, its purpose and its general running. It is mosty likely going to be in a state of continuous beta, however it is intended to capture the general gist what MAN-UP is all about.

Mantra
  • We want to create stuff that is worth talking about. Things that not only someone familiar with computer science could appreciate but also someone forom outside the field. To make computer science exciting.
  • There are two types of things that can be created in this world: things that are cool and things that are well implemented. We want to be able to createv things which are both well implemented and cool. As impossible as this seems.
  • When it comes down to it, sometimes you've just got to make the thing yourself.
  • Someone out there is better than you and has done more interesting things. Never assume you're the best - but at least give it a go.
  • It's all about having a good story to tell at the end.

Goals
  • We want to create a sense of friendly competition between the members of the Society
  • Each member has associated with them XP, which is represented on their profile on this site
  • XP is earnt through badges.
  • Badges are awarded by completing certain nerdy tasks
  • Members then get to showcase what they have done to earn their badges at the meetings

Activities

There are three main activities that are organised by the society

  1. Meetings
  2. Master-classes
  3. Hack-a-thons

Meetings

These take place every Thursday at 5pm in LF15, in the Kilburn Building (you might want to check the calendar to double check).

The general outline for what goes in meetings is

An interactive showcase introduction

Member/members of society volunteer to entertain whilst the room fills up. The source of this entertainment could be anything which is fun and has some form of technical value.

This week in Tech

Where we get to discuss the latest happenings in technology such as what exciting things have been happening in Silicon Valley or in Academia.

Question of the week

This an open-ended, tongue in cheek question that is designed to for everyone in the group to have fun answering it, e.g., "if you could be any cable, what would you be, and what gender would your connectors be?".

Talks / Open Mic for nerds

This is the open session for any members who want to show off things that they have created. Essentially it's a five minute slot in front of the group. You can email in advance to let us know you want to give a talk, or just say on the day, All are welcome. It is advised you bring your own laptop as there is one school computer in the room, but we don't trust it.

Hack-a-thon planning/review

Where we go over organisational details for upcoming hack-a-thons and also reflect on a hack-a-thon after it has taken place - figuring out what worked and what bombed.

Badge awards

The most exciting part of them all, where we hand out the hard earnt badges. There will be tears.

Master Classes

These are on Wednesday lunchtimes (again see the calendar) for confirmation. These are where a member of the society gives a talk on something that they have knowlegde of that they think others should know.

They are also designed to prepare members with the tools/skills needed for upcoming hack-a-thons.

Hack-a-thons

This is where the real programming happens. Hack-a-thons are competitions held roughly every two weeks (once again check the calendar). You will be able to work as part of a team to create a solution that will be pitted against those created by other teams in an attempt to win a Reality Mug - the most coverted prizes of all time.

Badges

What are badges?

These are the Life Force of MAN-UP, the items that once earnt will reward you with precious XP, allowing you to climb the ranks of the MAN-UP leaderboard.

Of course they are a little bit tongue-in-cheek. Please do not take the descriptions of tasks that they demand of you to be verbatim, it is more the general spirit of task that is to be done.

They are divided into different categories, ranging from the serious to the absurd and depending on the difficulty of the task they have different XP rewards.

How to claim a badge

Quite simply go to the badge application form and pick the badge you wish to claim for and post a link to your proof. We recommend hosting a tar of your proof, which contains any source code, images or links to video, in your public Dropbox folder or blog. If you don't have Dropbox, you should, get it here.

Code of Honour

Quite simply, it would be easy to cheat and lie about badges. As we are only a small society there is relatively very little point in doing this - as we will find you and exile you.

So in brief, here is our code of honour

  • Don't be Scott Jamie
